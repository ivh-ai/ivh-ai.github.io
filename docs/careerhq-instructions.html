How to use

Using Career HQ

Career HQ is a job-search command center: one dashboard for the pipeline, resumes, interview prep, and research, powered by Claude behind the scenes. This guide tours the interactive demo, then explains how the system runs a real search.

Quick start

Open the demo → click through the six sections in the sidebar → open a pipeline card → try dragging one between stages. Everything you see is fictional sample data, and the demo is read-only.

Touring the dashboard

  1. Overview — the whole search at a glance

    The landing view answers "how is the search going?" in one screen: open applications, how many are in interview stages, offers on the table, and this week's activity with a response rate. Below that: a stage-by-stage pipeline funnel, the next actions list with due-date badges (overdue items in red), a weekly activity chart, a donut of where applications come from, best-fit roles ranked by fit score, and a shelf of research documents.

  2. Pipeline — the kanban board

    Every application is a card in a stage column: identified → applied → screen → interview → final → offer, plus a closed archive. Click any card to open its detail drawer, and try dragging one to another column — in the real system that drag rewrites the tracker file on disk. Cards show fit scores and due dates at a glance.

  3. Resume Studio

    Shows the profile's readiness (master resume, positioning, story bank) and collects the tailored one-page resumes generated per job posting. The prompt cards below are how new ones get made — more on those in a moment.

  4. Job Search

    Where new opportunities enter the system. The "Identified — not yet applied" list holds roles under consideration with their fit scores, locations, and salaries. The prompt cards ask Claude to search connected job boards and rank results against the resume.

  5. Interview Prep

    Lists every application currently in an interview stage with its next action and due date, alongside the per-company prep packs — research briefs with likely questions mapped to real stories.

  6. Compare Roles & Industry Intel

    The decision-support sections: head-to-head role comparisons weighted by personal priorities (ending in an actual recommendation), and saved industry deep dives and company research briefs.

How it runs a real job search

The dashboard is the window; Claude is the engine. Scattered through every section are prompt cards — each has a "Copy prompt" button. In real use, the workflow is:

  1. Copy a prompt, paste it into Claude

    "Find jobs that match my profile," "Tailor my resume to this posting," "Prep me for my final-round interview at [company]" — each prompt triggers a purpose-built Claude skill that already knows the rules of the system.

  2. Claude does the work and writes it down

    The skill reads the profile, does the searching / tailoring / prep, and saves its output as plain markdown files — which means the dashboard reflects it instantly, since both read the same files.

  3. Everything gets logged

    Every application, follow-up, and interview lands in the tracker automatically — the funnel, charts, and response rate on the Overview are computed from that log. A weekly review prompt walks the whole pipeline and flags what's stalling.

Why the demo is read-only

The real Career HQ runs locally on private career data. The demo swaps that for fictional sample data, so edit actions show a friendly explanation instead of writing anything. For a live walkthrough of the real system, get in touch.
