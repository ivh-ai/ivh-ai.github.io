Habit Tracker

A habit tracker that respects your data: install it on your phone like a native app, track daily and weekly habits with streaks, heatmaps, and motivational nudges — and every byte stays on your device. No account. No server. No sync to anyone's cloud.

  • React
  • TypeScript
  • Tailwind CSS
  • Recharts
  • PWA
  • Vitest

The idea

Habit apps tend to fail in one of two ways: they demand an account and quietly own your data, or they're so bare-bones they stop being motivating. The goal here was a tracker that's genuinely pleasant to open every day and completely private:

  • Local-first — all data lives on the device; a JSON export/import moves it between devices
  • Installable — works as a real app on a phone home screen, fully offline
  • Two kinds of habits — simple check-offs ("Meditate") and quantity targets ("8 glasses of water")
  • Real scheduling — habits can run every day or specific weekdays, and stats only count scheduled days
  • Motivation built in — streaks, a year-long heatmap, weekly reports, and nudges that notice when you're slipping
In plain English

How it works

Your day at a glance

The Today screen lists what's scheduled, with a progress ring filling up as you check things off. Quantity habits count up tap by tap — five glasses down, three to go. Each card shows the habit's live streak, which is exactly the number you won't want to break.

The gentle nudge

When you open the app, it quietly checks how you're doing. A 9-day meditation streak with today still unchecked? It says so. A rough week compared to your average? It notices that too — and when you're at 100%, it celebrates. One message at a time, picked by priority, so it never nags.

The long view

Reports compare this week to last week, habit by habit. The Dashboard zooms out further: a completion trend line, per-habit percentages, a GitHub-style heatmap of your whole year, and a streak leaderboard. Progress you can actually see is the strongest habit-building mechanic there is.

Your data, literally yours

Everything is stored in your browser on your device. Nothing is uploaded anywhere. Settings has one-click export to a JSON file (and import on a new device) — and if stored data ever gets corrupted, the app detects it, warns you, and offers recovery instead of silently wiping your history.

For technical readers

Under the hood

Storage & data model

All state lives in localStorage under a single versioned key (habit-tracker:v1), accessed only through a typed store module — so a future schema migration or IndexedDB swap touches one file. The model is two arrays: habits (id, type, optional target/unit, scheduled weekdays, createdAt, soft-delete archivedAt) and entries (habitId, date, value) — absence of an entry means "not done." Every load runs full shape validation; corrupted data falls back to a safe empty state with a visible warning, never a silent overwrite.

Pure-function core, fully unit-tested

Streaks, completion math, week/month/quarter/year aggregation, and the nudge engine are pure functions in lib/ with Vitest coverage of the edge cases: weekday schedules, archived habits, period boundaries, and export/import round-trips. Completion only counts days that are on/after createdAt, before archivedAt, and actually scheduled — so skipping Saturday can't break a Mon/Wed/Fri streak.

The nudge engine

A single priority-ordered function (habits, entries, today) → nudge | null: streak-in-danger beats week-slump beats habit-gone-cold beats positive reinforcement. Message templates are picked by hashing the date, so wording varies day to day without any stored state.

PWA & rendering

vite-plugin-pwa generates the manifest and a Workbox service worker that precaches the whole app — installable on iOS and Android, fully functional offline. Charts (trend line, weekly bars) are Recharts; the heatmap and progress ring are hand-rolled SVG. Styling is Tailwind CSS with system-preference dark mode.

About this demo

The demo on this site is the real production build. A small seed script writes ~6 weeks of sample data on first visit — only if no data exists — so the charts, streaks, and nudges look alive. Anything you check off is stored in your browser: the demo is the local-first architecture demonstrating itself.

Key design decisions

Local-first, no accounts

Habit data is intimate. Keeping it on-device removes the privacy question entirely and makes the app free to run forever — the tradeoff (no automatic sync) is bridged by JSON export/import.

Scheduled days, honest stats

A Mon/Wed/Fri running habit shouldn't be "missed" on Sunday. Stats and streaks only count scheduled days, which keeps the numbers truthful and the motivation fair.

Test-driven core

Date and streak logic is where habit trackers quietly lie to users. Building the pure-logic layer with unit tests first (TDD) meant the edge cases — timezones, week boundaries, archived habits — were handled before any UI existed.

Ruthless v1 scope

Notifications, cross-device sync, per-habit colors, and timed habits were all explicitly cut. A tracker you actually open daily beats a feature list — the cut list is written down for v2.
